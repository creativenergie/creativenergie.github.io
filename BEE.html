Bitesize Energy Exchange

Enabling surplus clean energy to be shared easily within communities: packaging bitesize quantities of solar power and biogas fuel for peer-to-peer distribution and trading.

The Challenge

Solar panels and biogas digesters provide essential sources of light, electricity and cooking fuel in Sub-Saharan Africa. Yet the poorest African families remain unable to afford them, excluding them from accessing vital clean energy sources. At the same time some of their neighbours have more energy than they need: greater solar power is generated than consumed during the day; biogas systems continually produce methane gas and the excess is wasted.

What are we doing about it?

CREATIVenergie and partners are championing fairer ways to share clean energy supplies, leading to transformative change for every community member. Together we have won an Energy Catalyst award, with DfID providing the majority of the project’s £700k budget, to enable us to harness currently wasted solar power and biogas fuel, which will have life changing impacts for people currently living in energy poverty. Starting in Uganda and Tanzania, the BEE Project will use proven technologies to enable excess solar power and excess biogas fuel to be packaged, distributed and traded in bitesize quantities. People already trade other excess produce, ranging from milk to eggs, in small packages. The BEE project brings this concept to energy.

Impacts

  • Increase access to reliable supplies of affordable, clean energy for poorer families who are not able to afford the assets to generate such energy by enabling surplus energy to be easily shared and existing assets used to full potential;
  • Provide clean cooking fuels to replace traditional smoky fuels, reducing breathing in of smoke and improving health;
  • Provide light for studying, working and keeping safe during the evenings;
  • Increased return on investment for asset owners (trading rather than wasting surplus energy);
  • Create local enterprise opportunities leading to income-generation.
